Chapter 1.

Introduction to
IP Cameras

"Surveillance cameras," also known as "Network cameras", "Internet Protocol cameras" and "IP cameras" evolved from the digital video camera. With the capability of transferring data via a computer network and the internet, these cameras set a new standard for the surveillance industry. In this chapter, you will learn the difference between IP cameras and analog cameras and why the IP camera is the best choice when building a reliable and cost-effective surveillance system.

Chapter 2.

System Architecture

In an IP surveillance system, video streams are digitized and transmitted over networks. This allows users to view and manage their video and images remotely with networked device such as a PC at without limits on location or time. In this chapter, we will introduce the types of components that you will need and how to build up your own surveillance system, step-by-step.

Chapter 3.

Introduction to Networks

IP cameras and computers can be connected via the same Ethernet network. In this chapter, you will learn the computer networking basics for surveillance systems.

Chapter 4.

Network
Bandwidth

Since IP cameras transfer digitized video signals via a network, understanding bit-rate and bandwidth allocation control is essential. In this chapter, you will learn the relationship between bit-rate and bandwidth, how to transmit videos over an IP-based network and how to store video data in a cost effective manner.

Chapter 5.

Different Types of Cameras

A network camera can be designed for indoor use only, or for both indoors and outdoor use. In this chapter, we will introduce different types of cameras and accessories, including the enclosures you may need to protect your cameras and the mounting kits which allow for installation in a wide variety of locations. You will learn the advantages of different types of cameras, and know how to find out the necessary accessories for your application.
