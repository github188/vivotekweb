Introduction

Continuous operation of VIVOTEK cameras is a key element in the protection of assets and providing safety. Some of the most unpredictable interruptions to the proper operation of camera are lightning strikes. The currents produced by surges can be carried over any metallic conductor. Proper installation of surge protection is the best way to protect your camera.

What is a Power Surge?

Power Surges occurs when something boosts the electrical charge at some point in the power lines. This can cause an increase in the electrical potential energy, which can increase the current flowing to your outlet or PoE switch. On this particular scenario a number of different things can cause this happen.

The most familiar source is probably lightning, though it is actually one of the least common causes. When lightning strikes near a power line, whether it is underground, in building or running along poles the electrical energy can increase electrical pressure of volts. This causes an extremely large power surge that will overpower almost any PoE switch.

How to have a proper installation of surge protection?

VIVOTEK cameras have passed in CE lightning protection standards. However, the protection should not be enough if customer put the camera in high-risk environments.

To improve lightning protection standards, we recommend to put an external surge protector between PoE switch cable and cameras cable. As much as possible use the Shielded Twisted Pair (STP). Aluminum Mylar Shield (refer to Figure 1) helps protect the data from any sort of electromagnetic interference from an outside source. This could alter the signal traveling through the copper in the cable.

Another approach is by placing an external metal (angle iron or steel tube) which will serve as a grounding conductor. The lightning conductor must be in the highest point so that it attracts the strike away from the cameras and equipment. Grounding line should be 8mm in diameter with steel wire or pole wherein the camera should be mounted.

Running cable in parallel with electrical cable - Data cabling used Unshielded Twisted Pairs (UTP), the magnetic field generated by the low voltage running through the cable is a critical component of the communications chain. When you run this unshielded cabling in parallel with electrical cables, that magnetic field is disrupted and the communication becomes inaccurate.     1. Follow the building codes or ordinances - This is really important for many reasons. First of all, failure to adhere to local codes can create dangerous issues for safety personnel. For example, in most places, use of PVC-jacketed cabling is prohibited in air handling spaces. When PVC burns, it creates a toxic stew that can be harmful to firefighters and other personnel that might have to navigate the area in the event of an emergency. If you fail to follow local codes related to low voltage cabling, you risk fines and may even have to rip and replace your cabling. So make sure you verify your responsibilities before you get started and make sure that any contractors you have working with you are aware of local ordinances as well.
