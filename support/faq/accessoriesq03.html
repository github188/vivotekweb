Usage scenarios

A VIVOTEK wireless solution is ideal for the following scenarios:

  1. Scenario #1: It is too expensive to deploy the Ethernet cables between sites of interest.

    1. Objective: You wish to stream video from IP cameras in Buildings B and C to Building A, where a central control room is located.
    2. Problem: There is no Ethernet LAN that covers all three buildings, and to lay the necessary cables would be expensive-in Taiwan, for example, a fee of USD90 per meter applies for permission to excavate a road and lay Ethernet cable. For a total distance of 900 meters, the cost would amount to USD81,000.
    3. Solution: Install VIVOTEK's outdoor wireless access points on the roof of each of the three buildings to provide a wireless LAN that covers all three.

      The only equipment that needs to be purchased is the three access points, four antennas, and four RF cables, at a cost that would be far less than USD81,000, and making this solution highly cost-effective.
  2. Scenario #2: It is impossible to deploy Ethernet cables between two sites of interest.

    1. Objective: You wish to stream video from an IP camera to a central control room.
    2. Problem: There is a river separating the locations of the IP camera and the central control room, making it physically impossible to set up a wired network connection.
    3. Solution: Install VIVOTEK wireless outdoor access points at the two locations as shown:

      This wireless solution makes real-time video streaming to the central control room possible.
  3. Scenario #3: There is physical barrier (e.g. mountains or buildings) that blocks wireless signal transmission between two sites of interest.

    1. Objective: You wish to stream video from an IP Camera to a central control room.
    2. Problem: A mountain blocks transmission of wireless network signals between the location where the IP camera is installed and the central control room
    3. Solution: A 600AG (dual-radio access point) is installed at a third location to forward the wireless signal as shown:
      This wireless solution takes advantage of a daisy chain network. Refer to the next page for information about what a daisy chain network is.

Daisy chain network structure

A daisy chain enables video to be streamed from seven cameras whose locations span a distance of 30 kilometers, and for this video to be monitored from six locations.

  1. Throughput will be attenuated by 2Mbps at each node.
  2. After passing through the fifth node, throughput will be maintained at 12Mbps and no further attenuation occurs
