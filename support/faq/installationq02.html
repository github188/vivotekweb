Focus Assist

Introduction

VIVOTEK provides a number of megapixel cameras for customers requiring a high level of detail in their surveillance video. When the camera is installed, a mobile monitor attached via the camera's BNC connector or a Notebook attached via the camera's Ethernet connector is generally used to adjust the focus. Problems may arise, however, because video that appears to be in focus on the screen of a mobile monitor or a Notebook may not be so when viewed through a web browser by users. To resolve this issue, VIVOTEK has designed the Focus Assist feature to ensure that focus can be adjusted easily and correctly during installation.

This document explains the use of the Focus Assist feature.

Set the Iris mode before installing the camera

In order to adjust the focus with precision, the depth of field should be shortest by adjusting the camera's iris to the fully open state. Depending on the type of the iris used in the camera, the procedure is somewhat different. Before installing the camera, please connect the camera by IE and setup its iris to be fully opened.

  1. DC-Iris camera

    Go to Audio and video > Video Settings > Iris mode

    1. Set the Iris mode to Fixed (this will cause the iris to open fully)
  2. P-Iris camera

    Go to Audio and video > Video Settings > Iris mode

    1. Set the Iris mode to Manual
    2. Set the Iris adjustment value to 1 (this will cause the iris to open fully)

    1. When a mobile monitor attached via the camera's BNC connector is used to adjust the focus:

      Press the Focus Assist button on the back panel of the camera and then the mobile monitor will show the setting text on the screen from BNC Video out.

    2. When a Notebook attached via the camera's Ethernet connector is generally used to adjust the focus:

      Click the Focus Assist button "On" on the home page of the camera by IE and then the Notebook will show the setting text on the screen.

  4. The camera will activate the Focus Assist function and the image will show the text "XX/---", where the first parameter is the current value, and the second is the optimum value.
  5. After enabling the Focus Assist function, use the focus controller from the minimum to the maximum, allowing the camera to determine the optimum focus value. The view on the screen should now display numerical values for both parameters, as in the figure below.

  6. Use the focus controller to adjust focus so that the values of the two parameters are identical. The view on the screen will display the message "BEST FOCUS".
  7. Press the Focus Assist button once more or click Focus Assist button "Off" on the web page to remove the OSD text and complete the task of adjusting focus.


    NOTE: Before enabling the Focus Assist feature, the camera's position and view angle must be fixed, as described in steps 1 and 2, to ensure a stable view. If the view is altered, the Focus Assist feature will need to be restarted in order to determine the optimum value again. To restart it, please press the Focus Assist button once again or click Focus Assist button "Off" on the web page and repeat steps 1 to 7.
