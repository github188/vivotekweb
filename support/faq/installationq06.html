Introduction

We have received many reports from customers about leaking issues with their outdoor cameras, especially the IP8332 model. This document describes how to troubleshoot such issues.

Possible causes of leaking

Following are some questions to answer as you troubleshoot the cause of any leaking you’re experiencing

  1. Do you know which part of the camera is waterproof? On an IP66-rated outdoor camera, the camera itself and cable gland on the camera’s end are waterproof, but the cable molding on the other end is not. Check if the water is leaking from the cable molding into the camera.
  2. Is the camera installed correctly? Is the cable molding exposed? Check if it is protected from exposure to water, then use expanding foam sealant or putty to prevent water from leaking in.



    If the cable molding or network connector might be unavoidably exposed to water, you can use an AN3000 outdoor Ethernet Waterproof connector to prevent leaking.
  3. Check that the IP8332‘s back cover is correctly installed. Water may leak from the back cover into the camera. Check if it is securely tightened. We have a video clip on Youtube for your reference
  4. Check if the rubber ring and plastic on the front of the IP8332 are still present.
  5. Check if there is any gap between the cover and camera body. (MD7560/MD7530/MD8562)
  6. Don’t remove of waterproof gasket. (MD7560/MD7530/MD8562)

What to do if none of the fixes worked

  1. Take a photo of the camera itself as it is installed on site
  2. Take a photo showing the leaking issue on the inside of the camera
  3. Contact your vendor or our distributor for direct support
