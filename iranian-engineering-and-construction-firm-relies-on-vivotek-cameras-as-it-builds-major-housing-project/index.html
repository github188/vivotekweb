Iranian Engineering and Construction Firm Relies on VIVOTEK Cameras as it Builds Major Housing Project

Background

Kayson is a privately held engineering and construction firm that has developed and built numerous large-scale projects for customers both in Iran and abroad. One of its recent projects was the Dolat É Mehr housing estate in Iran's Kermanshah Province. With 4000 residential units, Dolat É Mehr is one of the largest and most quickly built mass housing projects ever undertaken in Iran.

For this massive project, Kayson built a staging area that needed to be monitored. This staging area included not only outdoor and indoor storage areas for construction material and equipment, but also temporary office space for managers, worker dormitories, and a kitchen and canteen. After consulting with Gostaresh Informatics which is a local system integrator of VIVOTEK's distributor Pooya Fara Negar, Kayson chose VIVOTEK cameras for its security system at the site.

Solution

A total of 58 VIVOTEK cameras were used for the security system, with several different models adopted, each chosen for specific qualities that made it suitable for a particular location and function. To monitor corridors inside the staging area's temporary buildings, the FD8134 and MD7560 were chosen, largely because of their cost-effectiveness. The wide angle of view in the fixed-dome FD8134 was another plus for the model in its role monitoring the full length of the corridors. The MD7560's two-megapixel sensor, with its ability to deliver extremely detailed images, made it useful for more sensitive areas where positive identification of any unauthorized personnel was a must.

For outdoor lots and also the kitchen, the IP8332 was used. Though the IP8332 was designed primarily for outdoor surveillance applications, Kayson found that it was also ideal for the site's busy kitchen, where the heat and varying lighting conditions made it like an outdoor environment. Whether monitoring outdoor and indoor areas, the camera's IP66-rated weatherproof enclosure helped protect the camera. Kayson was also drawn by the camera's excellent 1-Megapixel video resolution and ability to provide clear nighttime footage as well.

VIVOTEK's SD7323 has proven popular among customers worldwide who need to monitor large outdoor spaces, which this camera can handle thanks to its powerful 35x zoom lens. Kayson likewise took advantage of the SD7323's ability to capture sharp detail at great distances. The IP7361 was installed around entrances to the staging area, where its high two-megapixel resolution was ready to support positive identification of individuals or vehicle license plates when the need arose. Concealed wiring to thwart tampering, a weatherproof design, and day-and-night recording were other features that made VIVOTEK's network bullet camera a natural fit for the role.

Customer Feedback

The VIVOTEK cameras performed ably in providing high-quality surveillance video for Kayson during the Dolat É Mehr project, and the company anticipates deploying VIVOTEK cameras for security systems in future projects as well.
