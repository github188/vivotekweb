VIVOTEK Preserves Canada's Aboriginal Cultural and Heritage Centre

Background

Canada is one of the most multicultural, diverse countries in the world. In 1971, Canada was the first country in the world to adopt multiculturalism as an official policy, affirming the value and dignity of all Canadian citizens regardless of their racial or ethnic origins, their language, or their religious affiliation. The majority of the population of Canada is made up of Old World immigrants and their descendants, including Aboriginals, which is the fastest-growing segment of the Canadian population. In the province of Nova Scotia, where the Aboriginal population is comparatively lower than the rest of Canada, there is a much more pressing need for a community to preserve the Aboriginal history. The Glooscap Heritage Centre and Mi'kmaw Museum (GHC&MM) is a heritage centre where the culture and the identity of Mi'kmaq, the Aboriginal group of Nova Scotia, are honored.

The centre consists of a 9000 square feet multimedia theatre, museum and gift shop where the exhibits are displayed as well as a 4,700 square feet annex for meetings and special events. There are numerous visitors, volunteers and clients sharing the facility. The venue gets particularly crowded during days where there are events, festivals and other special occasions. The safety of all people on the premise is indeed the priority of the management. In April 2011, the centre decided to bring in a surveillance system in order to protect assets and monitor traffic throughout the facility. Then, FD8133, one of VIVOTEK comprehensive, well-designed products, had been selected by the members of the management.

Solution

VIVOTEK FD8133 is a compact fixed dome designed for indoor applications. The elegant, stylish housing and flexible installation design help the device blend in well with the interior décor and layout. For this project, the cameras are mounted mostly in the exhibition rooms to watch people traffic. Others are installed at the entrance of the annex building and inside the gift shop.

Equipped with a 1-Megapixel sensor enabling viewing resolution of 1280x800 at 30 frames per second (fps), VIVOTEK FD8133 delivers high-quality, high-resolution image footages. Thanks to the support of industry standard H.264 compression technology, the network bandwidth required by the video transmission can easily be conserved. The more bandwidth saved, the more cost-effective the solution. To provide even more cost-effectiveness, VIVOTEK FD8133 is designed with built-in MicroSD/SDHC card slot for on-board storage. Therefore, the device allows recording to take place independently on the camera itself. This stand-alone masterpiece is truly an all-in-one surveillance solution that meets a wide variety of requirements for indoor surveillance.

With VIVOTEK ST7501 software, the in-house 32-channel recording software from VIVOTEK included in the product package, the administrator is provided with a complete overview of all cameras installed in the facility. All compatible video standards, H.264, MPEG-4 and MJPEG, implemented on the camera allow the video stream to be individually configured and transmitted to meet different requirements and constraints for versatile applications. Thus, users can receive multiple streams simultaneously in different resolutions, frame rates and image qualities for monitoring on different platforms, depending on the need of the viewer.

Customer Feedback

VIVOTEK FD8133 was chosen because of the combination of price, performance, and appearance. "We are very happy with the VIVOTEK cameras," remarked General Manager Sharon Touchie. "Having high resolution video throughout the facility has been a huge boost for us in providing safety and security for our visitors when they come to learn about the richness of Mi'kmaw culture."
